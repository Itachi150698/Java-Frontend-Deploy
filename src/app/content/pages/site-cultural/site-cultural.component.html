Culture plays an important role in the development of any nation. It represents a set of shared attitudes, values, goals and practices. Culture and creativity manifest themselves in almost all economic, social and other activities. A country as diverse as India is symbolized by the plurality of its culture. India has one of the world’s largest collections of songs, music, dance, theatre, folk traditions, performing arts, rites and rituals, paintings and writings that are known, as the ‘Intangible Cultural Heritage’ (ICH) of humanity. In order to preserve these elements, the Ministry of Culture implements a number of schemes and programmes aimed at providing financial support to individuals, groups and cultural organizations engaged in performing, visual and literary arts etc. This section offers comprehensive information related to cultural heritage, ancient monuments, literary arts, visual arts, schemes, programmes, performing arts, fairs and festivals and handicraft of India. Detailed information on various organizations involved in promotion and propagation of Indian art and culture is also available in this section.

Heritage Sites

Sanchi Stupa

The Sanchi Stupa is an ancient Buddhist stupa and a prominent pilgrimage site. It is known for its remarkable preservation and intricate carvings, which provide valuable insights into early Buddhist art and architecture. The site includes several stupas, monasteries, and temples, showcasing the rich history of Buddhism in India.

Ruins of Hampi

The Ruins of Hampi are a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the southern part of India, in the state of Karnataka. Hampi, formerly known as Vijayanagara, was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire, which flourished between the 14th and 17th centuries. The site is renowned for its impressive ruins, which reflect the grandeur and architectural brilliance of the Vijayanagara Empire.

Read More

Agra Fort

Agra Fort, also known as the Red Fort of Agra, has a rich history intertwined with the rise and fall of the Mughal Empire. The fort was initially a brick fort known as "Badalgarh," held by the Rajputs. Emperor Akbar rebuilt it in red sandstone, giving it its current form. The fort witnessed several historical events, including the coronation of Emperor Shah Jahan and his imprisonment by Aurangzeb.

Read More

Ellora

Ellora, located in the Aurangabad district of Maharashtra, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its extraordinary rock-cut caves. These caves, dating back to between the 6th and 10th centuries CE, represent the pinnacle of Indian rock-cut architecture and are a testament to the religious harmony that existed during the period.

Read More

Khajuraho

Khajuraho was once the religious and cultural capital of the Chandela rulers, who were great patrons of art and architecture. The temples reflect the social, religious, and cultural life of the time. Built over a period of 100 years, only 22 of the original 85 temples have survived, showcasing the architectural genius of the Chandela dynasty.

Read More

Somnath Temple, Gujarat

The Somnath Temple, located in Prabhas Patan near Veraval in Gujarat, India, is one of the most revered and ancient temples dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is considered the first of the twelve Jyotirlingas, making it a significant pilgrimage site for Hindus. The temple's history is marked by its resilience, having been destroyed and rebuilt several times over the centuries.

Read More

Shore Temple, Mahabalipuram

The Shore Temple in Mahabalipuram, Tamil Nadu, is one of the oldest structural (stone) temples in South India, dating back to the 8th century during the reign of the Pallava dynasty. The temple complex, a UNESCO World Heritage site, consists of three shrines—two dedicated to Lord Shiva and one to Lord Vishnu.

Brihadeeswarar Temple

Also known as the "Big Temple," this Dravidian-style temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva. Located in Thanjavur, it is one of the largest temples in India and is a part of the UNESCO World Heritage site known as the "Great Living Chola Temples."

Tirumala Venkateswara Temple

Located in Tirupati, this is one of the most famous and richest temples in the world, dedicated to Lord Venkateswara (a form of Vishnu). The temple's golden dome and intricate carvings are iconic.

Taj Mahal

A symbol of love, the Taj Mahal in Agra is one of the most famous landmarks in the world. This white marble mausoleum was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal and is a UNESCO World Heritage site."
