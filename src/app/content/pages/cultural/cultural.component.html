Cultural Heritage of India

India, a nation rich in history and diversity, boasts a cultural tapestry woven from countless traditions, arts,
and culinary practices.

However, as the modern world rapidly evolves, many of these precious elements are fading away,
calling for a concerted effort to revive and preserve them for future generations.

Culture

India is a country with a diverse culture. India’s culture refers to a group of small distinct civilizations. Clothing, festivals, languages, religions, music, dance, architecture, food, and art are all part of Indian culture. Most notably, various foreign civilizations have affected Indian culture throughout its history. Furthermore, the history of Indian culture spans millennia. Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Sikhism are all religions. These religions are all founded on karma and dharma. These four are also known as Indian faiths. Along with Abrahamic religions, Indian religions constitute a major group of world religions. In addition, numerous foreign religions are practised in India. Abrahamic religions are among these foreign religions. In India, the Abrahamic religions are unquestionably Judaism, Christianity, and Islam. Other foreign religions in India include Zoroastrianism and the Bahá’ Faith, in addition to Abrahamic religions. As a result of the presence of so many different religions, Indian society has embraced tolerance and secularism.

Odissi

Originating from Odisha, Odissi is known for its lyrical, graceful movements and is often performed as a form of devotion to Hindu deities, particularly Lord Jagannath. The dance is characterized by its soft, rounded movements, complex rhythms, and the use of sculpturesque poses.

Gudi padwa

Gudi Padwa is a spring festival observed on the initial day of the Chaitra month in the Hindu calendar. Predominantly celebrated in Maharashtra and Goa, the festival signifies the commencement of the Hindu New Year according to the lunisolar calendar. The term "gudi" refers to a dhwaj (flag), and "padwa" is derived from "pratipada" or "prathama," signifying the first day of the bright phase of the moon.

Onam

The festival of Onam is celebrated throughout the state of Kerala. Kerala during Onam is marked by happiness, excitement and enjoyment among all sections of people. Onam is celebrated as an outcome of reasons that have to do with mythology as well as old agrarian practices. If one is to go by the myth, then King Mahabali or Maveli was a generous and virtuous ruler, who had once ruled Kerala. During his rule, the kingdom became so prosperous that devas (gods of the Heaven) felt jealous about this and also for the reason that King Mahabali was an asura - a member of the demon clan - who were the enemies of devas.

Kathakali

Kathakali, also from Kerala, is a classical dance-drama known for its elaborate costumes, detailed makeup, and colorful face masks. The dance is characterized by its vigorous and dramatic performances, which often depict stories from the Mahabharata and Ramayana. Kathakali is traditionally performed by male dancers.

Kathak

The word "Kathak" is derived from the Sanskrit word "Katha," meaning story, and "Kathakar," meaning the storyteller. Traditionally, Kathak dancers were the narrators of religious stories and mythologies, using a combination of dance, music, and mime to convey the narratives.

Raksha Bandhan

Raksha Bandhan is a popular festival celebrated across the entire country. People from all classes, beyond caste and religion, participate in this festival. It is celebrated on the full moon day of the lunar month of Shravan (Shravan Purnima), which also coincides with the observance of Upakarma (changing of the sacred thread for Brahmins, Avani Avittam in South India). This festival is also known as Rakhi Purnima, Nariyal Purnima, and Kajari Purnima in different states and is celebrated in various ways..

Phagu Sendra

Phagu Sendra stands as a communal hunting festival observed by the Oraon and Munda tribal communities, predominantly in Jharkhand and certain areas of Chhattisgarh. Timed on the eve of Falgun Purnima, corresponding to February-March in the Hindu Lunar calendar for the Oraons, and starting on the first day of the Hindu month of Chaitra (March-April) for the Mundas, the festival spans from a single day to a fortnight in various tribal villages across Jharkhand.

Hailing from Andhra Pradesh, Kuchipudi is a classical dance form known for its fast rhythms and fluid movements. It often includes a combination of dance, acting, and narration, which sets it apart from other classical dances.

Bhavai

Bhavai is a traditional folk dance from Rajasthan, celebrated for its extraordinary balancing acts. Dancers, often from the Bhil community, perform by skillfully balancing multiple pots, glass bottles, or even burning lamps on their heads while executing graceful, intricate movements. The dance is a vibrant showcase of the endurance and strength of Rajasthani women, reflecting their daily lives where they carry water over long distances. Accompanied by traditional folk music, Bhavai is performed during festivals, weddings, and cultural events, captivating audiences with its mix of artistry and agility. It is a significant part of Rajasthan’s rich cultural heritage.

Khasi

That the Khasi Hills Autonomous District Council (KHADC) would lash out with a legislation, as it did on July 25, 2018, to curb marriages between women of their tribe and men of other communities, was inevitable. In the hills, fatwas by village durbars or NGOs aimed at restricting alliances between a Khasi woman and a non-Khasi man are neither new nor unusual. But the introduction of the recent legislation is set to divide opinions and exacerbate the conflict even more. At the heart of the issue, which has been rabidly gnawing at the tribe for decades, is the suspicion that an alliance between a Khasi woman and a non-Khasi man is mere social contract that will allow the outsider male to exploit the tribal status of the Khasi woman.

Manipuri dance, originating from the northeastern state of Manipur, is one of the classical dance forms of India. It stands out for its lyrical and graceful movements, deeply intertwined with the cultural and spiritual life of the Manipuri people.

Kuttiyam

Kuttiyam, often referred to as Kuttiyum Kolum, is a traditional Indian game originating from Kerala. It is similar to Gilli-danda, where players use a small stick (kutti) and a longer stick (kol) to strike the kutti, aiming to flip it into the air and hit it as far as possible.

Naga Dance

The Naga dance is a vibrant and culturally rich dance form practiced by the Naga tribes of Northeast India, particularly in the state of Nagaland. The Naga people are known for their deep connection to their traditions, and their dances are a significant expression of their cultural identity. The Naga dance is not just a form of entertainment but a symbolic representation of the community’s history, beliefs, and way of life.

Mohiniyattam (Mohiniattam) is a classical dance form of Kerala in South India. The origin and popularity of this dance form are closely linked to the great Tamil dance master Vadivelu, one of the Thanjavur quartets.

Folk Dance of Punjab

Bhangra is a vibrant and energetic folk dance from Punjab, traditionally performed to celebrate the harvest season. The dance involves vigorous movements, high jumps, and enthusiastic gestures, usually accompanied by the dhol (a double-headed drum) and other musical instruments. Bhangra has now gained popularity worldwide.

Kalaripayattu

Considered among the oldest and most scientific martial arts in the world, Kalaripayattu was developed in Kerala. Lauded as the pride of Kerala, it is acknowledged and respected across the world. The training begins with an oil massage of the entire body until it is agile and supple. Feats like chattom (jumping), ottam (running) and marichil (somersault) are also integral parts of the art form. There are also lessons in using weapons like swords, daggers, spears, maces, and bows and arrows.

Jaisalmer Desert Festival

The Jaisalmer Desert Festival is a vibrant, annual cultural event that takes place in the heart of the Thar Desert, Rajasthan, usually in February. Celebrating the rich heritage of the region, the festival spans over three days, transforming the golden sands into a colorful extravaganza. Visitors can witness traditional Rajasthani folk music and dance performances, including the mesmerizing Ghoomar and Kalbeliya dances.

Manipuri classical

Manipuri classical dance is a traditional classical dance form that originated in the northeastern Indian state of Manipur. It is known for its grace, fluidity, and delicate movements. These are inspired by the rich cultural traditions and religious practices of Manipur. Manipuri dance is deeply rooted in the Vaishnavite tradition. It is often performed as a devotional offering to Lord Krishna. The dance form is characterized by its gentle footwork, hand gestures (mudras), expressive facial expressions, and elaborate costumes that include vibrant traditional attire and intricate jewelry.
